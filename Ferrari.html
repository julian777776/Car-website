Ferrari 250 GT California

The 1963 Ferarri 250 GT California was made about 60 years ago, but it still holdsup today. The car is famous for starring in Ferris Buellers Day Off, and sold for a record breaking 11 million dollars.

Ferrari F50

The Ferrari F50 is a sports car manufactured Ferrari from 1995-1997. Introduced in 1995, the car is a two-door, two seat targa top, and only a total of 349 cars were made with the last car rolling off the production line in July 1997.

Ferrari LaFerrari Spyder

The LaFerrari, project name F150 is a limited production hybrid sports car built by Ferrari. LaFerrari means "The Ferrari" in Italian and some other Romance languages, in the sense that it is the "definitive" Ferrari. Production is limited, so the car can reach an easy 1 million dollars

Ferrari Dino

Named to honour Ferrari founder Enzo Ferrari's son and heir Dino Ferrari, at the time, the car was suppossed to be a cheap and affordable option on the Ferrari car market. Today, it is recognized as an icon to Ferrari.
